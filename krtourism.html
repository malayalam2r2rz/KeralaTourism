Vallam Kali is a traditional boat race in Kerala, India.
It is a form of canoe racing, and uses paddled war canoes.
It is mainly conducted during the season of the harvest festival Onam in spring.
Vallam kali includes races of many kinds of paddled longboats and 'snake boats'.

Hinduism and Islam are the two major religions of Kerala.
More than 50% of people in Kerala follow Hinduism, and more than 25% follow Islam.
Around 20% are Christians, and others follow Buddhism, Jainism, Judaism and other religions.
The cultural diversity of Kerala is well known.
